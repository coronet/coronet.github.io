David likes explaining complicated things in simple words. He's passionate about beautiful APIs, low-latency networking, highly-available distributed systems, and NoSQL databases.

Education

In 2007, he got a B.S. in C.S. from the University of Wisconsin - Madison.

Work

In 2007, David joined the Windows Networking team at Microsoft as an SDE. He contributed to the System.Net classes in the .NET Framework, and built the low-level networking APIs for Microsoft Silverlight. He learned a lot about asynchronous programming models, the HTTP protocol, and windbg. He engaged with customers building low-latency and large-scale networked systems to help them optimize performance and resource utilization.

In 2010, he left to join the E-Commerce Platform Services team at Amazon.com. He built a highly-available revision-controlled database for business rules, growing it from a single-tenant prototype built on a successor of Dynamo to a company-wide hosted service built on top of a durable distributed log (which he led the development of). He learned way too much about the Java garbage collector, the CAP theorem, and how to avoid getting paged at 2am. By the time he left, the service was used by ~100 teams and serving hundreds of thousands of requests per second.

Since 2013 he's worked for Amazon Web Services, where he contributes to AWS's open-source developer tools, engages with customers building performant and scalable applications on top of AWS, helps service teams design developer-friendly APIs, and occasionally gets paid to talk about his cat in public.
